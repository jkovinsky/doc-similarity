Dolphins are not only intelligent but also incredibly adept at learning and problem-solving. They have demonstrated the ability to learn from both observation and direct instruction, quickly adapting to new situations and challenges. In captivity, dolphins have been taught to perform a variety of tasks and tricks, showcasing their capacity for understanding commands and learning new behaviors. In the wild, they use their problem-solving skills to navigate their environment, locate food, and avoid dangers. This adaptability is a testament to their cognitive prowess and their ability to thrive in diverse conditions.
